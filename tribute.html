Gabriel Garcia Marquez

The "One Hundred Years of Solitude" author

Here is a timeline of Gabriel Garcia Marquez life:

  • 1927 - Born on March 6 in Aracataca, Colombia.
  • 1940 - Garcia Marquez moves to Barranquilla, a port city famous for its Carnival, to start high school.
  • 1947 - Studies law at the National University in Bogota and has two short stories published in the El Espectador newspaper.
  • 1950 - He works as a reporter and starts writing his first novel, “Leaf Storm.”
  • 1967 - “One Hundred Years of Solitude” is published in June.
  • 1979 - He divides his time between Colombia and Mexico.
  • 1982 - Garcia Marquez wins the Noble Prize for Literature.
  • 1987 - “Love in the Time of Cholera” is published.
  • 1994 - Establishes the Foundation for New Ibero-American Journalism to promote democracy and independent journalism in Latin America.
  • 1996 - “News of a Kidnapping”, a non-fiction account of the kidnapping is published.
  • 1999 - Battles lymphatic cancer, which goes into remission after chemotherapy treatment.
  • 2004 - Publishes his memoir “Living to Tell the Tale” in 2002.
  • 2012 - He is suffering from dementia and can no longer write.
  • 2014 - Garcia Marquez dies at his home in Mexico City.
“What matters in life is not what happens to you but what you remember and how you remember it.”

-- Gabriel Garcia Marquez
